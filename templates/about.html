About

Welcome to my Website! My name is Theo Lincke and I built this website to showcase some of the projects I've been working on in school and in my life in general.

What You'll Find

Under the navigation bar, you'll find a few different tabs. In the projects tab, you'll find three separate pages, electronics, math and programming. These are where I will be actually showcasing my projects. Most projects will begin with an introduction on the lessons, the process and some of the goals towards the project. Each will go into detail on the process of building the project and may contain some applets that help the user understand the project more. I encourage you to explore some topics that you never thought you would be interested in. Or if you find something that intrigues you, please feel free to contact me. I won't get much traffic on this site, so I'll be able to respond very quickly and I always love working on collaborations with other people. To contact me, please leave your name and email under My Contact's Page. Finally, you'll find an Affiliates tab. These are all of the organizations I am a part of. Check them out, especially if you're at Boulder right now. I would give them a lot of credit for providing me resources and ideas for each of my projects

GitHub

For every project that involves code, there will hopefully be a working link at the bottom titled GitHub Code. If you are interested in the project and would like to contribute, feel free to do so. The link will be at the bottom of each article. Collaboration and sharing of ideas is definitely the best way to improve a project so I've tried my best to comment out most of my code, making it easy to follow. If you have any questions about the code, contact me on my contact page or add an issue to the code.

A Note on Errors

I think any programmer goes through a certain phase in their life where their projects begin to be showcased to the public domain. During this transition, they realize just how important actually making their code run is. In class and in projects, I often find myself writing programs, stress testing them a bit, then finishing them. However, when your code is visible to everybody on the planet, you realize that every part of your code should be working inside and out. Thus, it is my hope that my website is running as smoothly as possible, but you're feedback is highly sought. If you find that something doesn't work right on the website, or it crashes unexpectedly, or if you know anything about web development and would like to share with me some of the atrocious mistakes I've made, please be my guest!

Enjoy!
